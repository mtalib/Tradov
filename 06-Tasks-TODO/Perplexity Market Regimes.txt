Perplexity - Market Regimes 

 In trading, people commonly use 2 to 4 practical regimes depending on how they classify them: trending vs. ranging, or trending, ranging, high-volatility, and low-volatility.

Common ways traders split regimes
2 regimes: momentum/trending vs. mean-reverting/ranging.

4 regimes: trending, sideways, high volatility, low volatility.

Broader models: some traders also add liquidity, risk appetite, or macro policy states, which makes the number much larger.

Practical answer
For most trading decisions, the useful answer is: there are a few core regimes, not a fixed number. The exact count depends on whether you define regimes by price direction, volatility, or market conditions like liquidity and macro environment.

A simple starting framework is:

Trend.

Range.

High volatility.

Low volatility.

SPY options Algo trading
Completed 2 steps
SPY options algo trading usually means automating entries and exits on SPY option contracts using signals from the underlying ETF, often with trend, momentum, volatility, or market-structure filters. In practice, traders use setups like 0DTE intraday bots, 2–5 DTE momentum systems, or rule-based pullback strategies with strict risk controls.

Common approaches
Trend following: EMA or MACD-style systems on SPY or SPY’s 1-minute/5-minute chart, then buying calls or puts when direction confirms.

Momentum reversal: RSI or oversold/overbought logic to fade short-term extremes.

Market structure: break-of-structure, pullback, and rejection-candle entries for directional intraday trades.

Regime filtering: only trade when the market is trending, volatile, or liquid enough, and avoid choppy conditions.

What matters most
For SPY options, execution quality matters as much as the signal because bid/ask spreads, fill price, and contract liquidity can dominate results. Good bots usually include position sizing, max daily loss rules, stop-loss logic, and realistic fill assumptions in backtests.

Practical setup
A robust SPY options algo often has this pipeline:

Detect regime on SPY.

Generate a directional bias.

Choose the option contract by DTE, delta, and premium.

Enforce risk limits and cooldowns.

Execute with limit orders and exit rules.

One simple example is: trade 0DTE SPY calls only when SPY breaks above intraday structure, volatility expands, and your entry is near a pullback with a predefined stop.
