Integrating the Kelly Criterion into an autonomous SPY options system (especially with PySide6 and Python) is a powerful way to manage risk, but it requires careful handling of "maximum drawdown" and "edge estimation."

Since you are trading SPY options, your potential for 100% loss on a trade is real. Below is a production-grade Python implementation designed for your environment.

Python Implementation: KellySizer
This class calculates the Kelly fraction and applies a "Fractional Kelly" multiplier to keep your account safe from the high volatility of SPY.

Python

import numpy as np

class KellySizer:
    def __init__(self, fraction=0.25):
        """
        :param fraction: The Kelly multiplier (e.g., 0.25 for Quarter-Kelly).
                         Used to reduce volatility and account for estimation errors.
        """
        self.fraction = fraction

    def calculate_kelly(self, win_rate, avg_win, avg_loss):
        """
        Calculates the Kelly Criterion percentage.
        :param win_rate: Float (0.0 to 1.0)
        :param avg_win: Average profit amount (absolute)
        :param avg_loss: Average loss amount (absolute)
        """
        if avg_loss == 0: return 0
        
        # Win/Loss Ratio (R)
        r = avg_win / avg_loss
        
        # Kelly Formula: K% = W - [(1 - W) / R]
        k_percent = win_rate - ((1 - win_rate) / r)
        
        # Apply fractional Kelly and ensure it's not negative
        safe_k = max(0, k_percent * self.fraction)
        
        return safe_k

    def get_position_size(self, equity, win_rate, avg_win, avg_loss):
        """Returns the dollar amount to risk on the next trade."""
        k_fraction = self.calculate_kelly(win_rate, avg_win, avg_loss)
        return equity * k_fraction

# Example Usage for SPY Options:
# Strategy: 60% win rate, $400 avg profit, $200 avg loss
sizer = KellySizer(fraction=0.5) # Half-Kelly
risk_amount = sizer.get_position_size(10000, 0.60, 400, 200)

print(f"Optimal Risk Amount: ${risk_amount:.2f}")
Integrating with PySide6 & Wayland
Since you are using Wayland and PySide6, you likely have a GUI updating in real-time. Here is how to structure the data flow:

Thread Safety: If your Kelly calculations involve a large backtest history, run them in a QThread or QRunnable so they don't freeze your PySide6 UI.

Dynamic Inputs: Bind your win_rate and avg_win/loss to the real-time performance of your bot. As the bot trades, the Kelly size should update automatically.

Visualizing the Edge: Use a simple ProgressBar or Gauge in PySide6 to show the current "Kelly Fraction." If it hits 0%, your bot should automatically pause trading (as the math suggests no edge).

Important Considerations for SPY Options
The "Losing Everything" Factor: In options, if you buy a call and it expires worthless, your loss is 100%. If your avg_loss is equal to your entire position size, Kelly will scale you down aggressively.

Correlation Risk: If your system opens multiple SPY positions (e.g., a Call and a Credit Spread) at once, Kelly assumes they are independent. They aren't. In this case, you should sum the risk of all open SPY positions to stay within your total Kelly limit.

Slippage and Fees: SPY options are liquid, but at high frequencies, slippage can eat your "edge." Ensure your avg_win calculation subtracts commissions.
